About Project

In recent years, the global cancer burden has risen with the aging population, leading to a higher proportion of cancer-related deaths. Predicting cancer occurrence is crucial for cost reduction and improved survival rates. Genetic mutations significantly increase cancer risk, but analyzing thousands of genes is challenging.

Recent research employs deep learning and wearable sensors to predict cancer using gene and mutation data, considering individual genome variations. Complete gene pattern analysis is essential for understanding the risk in each cancer patient beyond specific gene associations.
